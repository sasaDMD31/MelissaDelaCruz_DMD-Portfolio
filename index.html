Dental Cleaning (Prophylaxis):

This is a routine procedure where a dental hygienist or dentist removes plaque, tartar, and stains from teeth. It helps prevent cavities, gingivitis, and periodontal disease.

Tooth Extraction:

Tooth extraction is the removal of a tooth from its socket in the bone. This procedure is necessary when a tooth is severely damaged, decayed, or causing overcrowding. Extractions can be simple or surgical, depending on whether the tooth is visible or impacted (stuck below the gum line). After extraction, proper care is needed to ensure healing and prevent complications such as infection.

Filling Cavities:

When a tooth has decay (a cavity), a dentist removes the decayed portion and fills the area with a filling material, such as amalgam, composite resin, or gold, to restore the tooth's integrity and function.
